Pie charts are used to compare values of a dimension as proportions or percentages of the whole. The data should be categorical.

  • Use qualitative colors for pie pieces if the labels indicate opposite and specific meanings. Example, Online/Offline or High/Medium/Low/Safe risk.
  • Start the pie chart at "12 o'clock" and move in a clockwise direction.
  • Ensure that the segments are ordered by size (largest to smallest).
  • Limit pie chart slices to ten, too many different values to be represented, the chart becomes illegible.
  • Avoid using gradient colors and 3-D charts.
